Hoofs (Small)

  • Split hoof with two toes that leave a distinct imprint
  • Similar to large hoofed animals but smaller in size
  • Often found in rocky, mountainous terrain

Mountain Goat

To help them climb, mountain goats have toes that spread when they step, creating a distinctive V shape at the top of their print.

Wild Hog

Wild boar tracks can be confused with deer but have wider, rounder, blunter toes that don't come to a point. A dew claw often rests slightly outside the print.
