Hoofs (Large)

  • Split hoof with two toes that leave a distinct imprint
  • Some have toes that curve forming a heart-shaped print
  • Others have toes that are rounded and leave a round or even square-shaped print
Flashcard Mode

Moose

Moose have two toes that curve into a heart shape. Large tracks (5-7"), with dew claws sometimes visible in snow.

Deer

Deer, like moose, have two toes that curve sharply together forming almost a heart shape print. The prints are smaller in size than a moose measuring 2-3.5".

Elk

Elk have rounder toes, not as sharply tapered as deer or moose. Tracks measure 3-5", between deer and moose sizes.

Bison

Bison have rounder toes, not tapered like deer. Their print is wide and round rather than heart-shaped, measuring 4.5-6".

Cow

Cow prints are often confused with bison since they share the same round shape and relative size. The easiest way to tell them apart is to know your surroundings.
